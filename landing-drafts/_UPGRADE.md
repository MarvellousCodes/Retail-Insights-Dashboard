## The conversion model (what the best pages do)
A visitor moves through 5 stages; the page must answer them IN THIS ORDER:
1. **Attention (5s):** the hero makes a shop owner understand *what it is* + *the outcome* within 5 seconds.
2. **Understanding:** what does it do, *specifically* — concrete, not vague.
3. **Credibility:** do I believe the claims — proof, numbers, trust signals.
4. **Fit:** is this for *my* situation — name the audience explicitly.
5. **Safety:** can I try without risk — low-friction, reassuring CTA.

The #1 mistake to fix: **feature-speak instead of outcome-speak.** Rewrite accordingly.

## The 10 upgrades to apply to every variant
1. **5-second hero.** Clear value prop above the fold + ONE primary CTA. If the headline is clever/abstract
   (e.g. "Ask your shop anything"), add a one-line clarifying sub-headline that says plainly what it is.
2. **Outcomes, not features.** Rewrite section headers + copy. e.g. "Margin Tracking" → "Stop selling below
   cost without knowing." "Pricing Alerts" → "Catch wrong prices before they cost you at the till."
3. **Credibility strip near the hero** (Stage 3) — a trust bar: "Works with your existing CBE / Pervasive
   POS" · "GALA · BWG · Musgrave compatible" · "In pilot with an Irish forecourt" · "Your data stays in
   Ireland (GDPR)". Surface a killer proof number high: "423 margin leaks found in one shop."
4. **Proof above the fold, never buried.** Lead with the quantified outcome (€7,500–10,000/yr recovered ·
   423 issues · 150,556 records analysed · 3–5 hrs/week saved).
5. **Name the audience (Fit).** "Built for independent Irish convenience stores & forecourts." Speak to
   their real world: deliveries, fuel, tobacco, symbol groups, weekly spreadsheet grind.
6. **Risk reversal / Safety CTA.** Add low-friction reassurance: "See your own data first" · "Free margin
   scan, no commitment" · "No new hardware. No changes to your POS." · "Set up in an afternoon."
7. **One primary CTA, repeated.** Pick ONE action (e.g. "Book a free margin scan" or "Get early access")
   and repeat it; a single secondary ("See how it works"). No competing CTAs.
8. **Objection handling.** Add a short FAQ or inline reassurances answering: new hardware? (no) · data
   safe? (read-only AI, never leaves region) · expensive? (clear tiers + ~€1/mo AI cost) · hard to set up?
   (one afternoon) · works with my POS? (yes, CBE/Pervasive).
9. **Proven headline formulas.** Use one per variant: Promise ("Find €10k in lost margin — without touching
   your POS") · How-to ("Know exactly what's losing you money, in one afternoon") · Numbered · Outcome-CTA.
10. **Scannability + a strong closing CTA band.** Short benefit-led subheads, visual rhythm, decisive final CTA.

# RetailGuard Landing Page Recipes
## Sourced from WebAnatomy.ai benchmark (566 pages, 60+ conversion criteria, July 2026)

---

## === PRODUCT FACTS (RetailGuard) ===

Helps convenience store / forecourt owners find money left on the table by reading their till export. Features (plain terms, NEVER say "AI"): Overview dashboard (sales trends, margin health, top products/departments); Sales insights (pulse, same month last year by dept, top movers, department race, this week vs best week); Margins (margin ladder loss/thin/healthy/strong + "money left on the table" opportunities); Price moves (spots real price changes + profit impact, verdict on a proposed shelf price from that product's history, price picker, category sweep); Invoice scanner (snap a supplier invoice, matches lines to products, flags cost changes/mismatches); Ask your shop (plain-English question -> answer + chart + Excel export); supplier analytics; barcode lookup; Morning report (yesterday's margin anomalies by department, on the desk at open); Multi-site write-back (push price changes to back office, rolling out). Positioning: outcome-led, wedge is a FREE margin scan.

---

## === HARD RULES ===

1. NO em/en dashes anywhere (no -- or -, literal or entity). Use commas, full stops, or restructure.
2. NEVER use "AI"/"AI-powered"/"artificial intelligence".
3. Illustrative numbers ONLY, never a real shop's figures; label example numbers illustrative. Recovery figure as estimate: "Typical opportunities identified can amount to thousands of euros per year, depending on store size." No 53k headline, no ROI multiples.
4. No invented named testimonials/logos; any proof = illustrative example-shop outcomes clearly labelled, or a founder/pilot note.
5. Primary CTA = free margin scan, first-person copy ("Get my free margin scan") + friction reducer nearby ("Free. No card. Takes 2 minutes.").
6. Pricing exactly: Insight EUR500/mo (dashboard, alerts, drill-down, barcode, supplier analytics) / Complete EUR1,000/mo labelled Most popular and visually anchored (invoice scanner, ask your shop, trading patterns, product performance, priority support) / Multi-site EUR1,500/mo (everything + write-back, morning report, per-site onboarding). All "Net of VAT", "No setup fee".
7. Each page = ONE self-contained HTML file, FormSubmit AJAX to formsubmit.co/ajax/retailguard.sales@gmail.com with a unique hidden _subject per page, responsive to 360px, WCAG AA.

## Core WebAnatomy Findings (conversion patterns, not aesthetics)

**Meta-insight:** Clarity is the #1 predictor of hero score. Pages that stack 4-5 conversion patterns per section outscore pages that use 1-2, regardless of visual polish. Design serves copy, never the reverse. "No credit card required" outperforms countdown timers in 90% of top scorers.

### Per-section pattern library (named patterns + adoption rates from benchmark):

**HERO (313 scored):**
- Clear Value Proposition (89% adopt, table stakes)
- Visual Hierarchy (74%, headline > subhead > CTA flow)
- Product Visual (68%, real UI screenshot, not stock)
- Target Audience Clarity (61%, "For X" in first 5 seconds)
- Risk Reducer near CTA (42%, "No credit card" / "Free plan")
- Social Proof Above Fold (41%, logos/stat/quote before scroll)
- Differentiation (26%, big opportunity: "Unlike X" / "The only Y")
- Outcome Timeframe (21%, biggest gap: "results in 2 minutes")

**VALUE PROPOSITION (top-scoring pattern: Attribuly 100/100):**
- Multiple Propositions (separate cards per lever)
- Outcome Quantification (each card carries a number)
- Benefit Specificity (concrete, not adjective-driven)
- Unique Mechanism (name HOW, not just WHAT)

**FEATURES (top-scoring: SignOnSite 100/100):**
- Benefit-Led Copy (headline = outcome, not tool name)
- Pain-to-Outcome Mapping (contrast the old way vs new)
- Before/After Contrast (struck-through stat makes saving tangible)
- Outcome Visualization (real product screenshot showing the fix)
- Internal Linking (secondary features + deep-dive link)

**HOW IT WORKS (top-scoring: Finary 100/100):**
- Step Clarity ("Get started in 3 simple steps")
- Effort Perception ("totally free, takes 3 minutes")
- Timeline Specificity (concrete time commitment per step)
- Visual Progression (numbered steps + product mockup)
- Outcome Preview (step 3 names the payoff)
- Clear CTA right after steps

**PRICING (70 scored, 8 conversion practices):**
- Plan Clarity (100%, table stakes: one-liner per tier)
- Easy Comparison (90%, side-by-side columns)
- Anchoring Tags (73%, "Most Popular" badge does real work)
- Risk Reducer (60%, "Cancel anytime" near button)
- Value-Based Naming (56%, names map to aspirations)
- Annual/Monthly Toggle (31%, show savings)
- Calculator/Estimator (15%, biggest opportunity: interactive slider)
- Tooltip Explanations (6%, rarest but every one that has it is best-in-class)

**CTA (262 scored, 6 conversion practices):**
- Friction Reduction (89%, table stakes: "No credit card" / "Cancel anytime")
- Microcopy Quality (72%, supportive text addressing last doubt)
- Secondary Path (41%, "Watch demo" / "See pricing" for not-ready visitors)
- Consolidation (14%, one dominant action, no competing buttons)
- Urgency/Scarcity (12%, genuine only: manufactured fails)

**TESTIMONIALS (top-scoring: Lemlist 100/100):**
- Quantified Results (hard metrics headline each card)
- Company Logo Association (tie win to a known brand)
- Case Study Depth (full story link)
- Before/After Story (transformation concrete + time-bound)
- Keyword Highlights (bold metric callouts as eye-catch)

## RECIPE R1: "Clarity Standard"

**Strategy:** Clearest-page-wins. ProductLed/Freckle model. One specific hero claim + one dashboard mockup, benefit-led feature rows, anchored pricing, friction-free CTA.

**Section stack:** Navbar > Hero > Value Proposition > Features > How It Works > Pricing > Proof > FAQ > CTA > Footer

**Key section patterns:**

| Section | WebAnatomy Patterns (3-5) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Hero | Clear Value Proposition + Product Visual + Risk Reducer + Target Audience Clarity + Outcome Timeframe |
| Features | Benefit-Led Copy + Pain-to-Outcome Mapping + Outcome Visualization + Internal Linking |
| Pricing | Plan Clarity + Easy Comparison + Anchoring Tags + Risk Reducer + Value-Based Naming |
| CTA | Friction Reduction + Microcopy Quality + Consolidation + Secondary Path |

**Palette + type:** Deep violet (#4F46E5) primary, warm grey (#F9FAFB) bg, Inter/DM Sans. Clean whitespace, no grid background.

**Interactive element:** None. Static clarity. The page is the product demo.

**Hero copy direction:** Single specific claim ("Find the money your shop is already losing") + one RetailGuard dashboard screenshot (margin ladder) + "Get my free margin scan" + "Free. No card. Takes 2 minutes."

## RECIPE R2: "Proof and Numbers"

**Strategy:** Every section carries a quantified outcome. Before/after framing everywhere. Metric callouts + labelled illustrative example-shop results strip.

**Section stack:** Navbar > Hero (with metric strip) > Problem (quantified pain) > Value Proposition (numbered levers) > Features (with before/after stats) > Proof (illustrative results cards) > Pricing > FAQ > CTA > Footer

**Key section patterns:**

| Section | WebAnatomy Patterns (3-5) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Hero | Outcome Quantification + Social Proof Above Fold + Clear Value Proposition + Differentiation |
| Value Prop | Multiple Propositions + Outcome Quantification + Benefit Specificity + Unique Mechanism |
| Proof | Quantified Results + Before/After Story + Keyword Highlights |
| CTA | Friction Reduction + Microcopy Quality + Secondary Path |

**Palette + type:** Navy (#1E293B) sections alternating with white, emerald (#10B981) accent for positive metrics, struck-through red for "before" numbers. Space Grotesk headings, Inter body.

**Interactive element:** None, but each feature row has a large metric callout (e.g. "37 margin issues found" crossed out to "3 remaining after review") labelled "illustrative example-shop".

**Hero copy direction:** Metric-first: "Illustrative: one shop found EUR4,200 in annual margin gaps in 2 minutes." Subhead: "Your till export already holds the answers. We just read it for you."

## RECIPE R3: "Show the Product"

**Strategy:** CSS-drawn RetailGuard UI mockups in hero and every feature. The product IS the proof. Pain-to-outcome mapping with before/after struck-through stats.

**Section stack:** Navbar > Hero (full-width product mockup) > Problem > Features (alternating image/text, each with a CSS UI mockup) > How It Works > Pricing > CTA > Footer

**Key section patterns:**

| Section | WebAnatomy Patterns (3-5) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Hero | Product Visual + Visual Hierarchy + Clear Value Proposition + Risk Reducer + Outcome Timeframe |
| Features | Outcome Visualization + Before/After Contrast + Benefit-Led Copy + Pain-to-Outcome Mapping + Internal Linking |
| How It Works | Step Clarity + Effort Perception + Visual Progression + Outcome Preview + Clear CTA |
| Pricing | Plan Clarity + Anchoring Tags + Easy Comparison + Value-Based Naming |

**Palette + type:** RetailGuard brand violet (#7C3AED) on white, subtle grey card borders, system-ui stack for speed. Each mockup uses the actual RetailGuard colour palette.

**Interactive element:** Each feature mockup is a CSS-only static "screenshot" (margins ladder with colour-coded bars, price-moves chart with verdict badge, invoice scan result table with flagged rows, morning report email preview).

**Hero copy direction:** Full-width dashboard mockup dominates. Small headline above: "See every margin gap before your supplier does." CTA overlaid at bottom of mockup.

## RECIPE R4: "Free-Scan Estimator"

**Strategy:** Interactive hero estimator (Alchemy calculator pattern). Visitor inputs unit cost, shelf price, weekly units and instantly sees margin %, weekly gain, and a "scan finds this, imagine a full shop" hook. Free-scan wedge CTA.

**Section stack:** Navbar > Hero (calculator) > Value Proposition > Features > How It Works > Pricing > FAQ > CTA > Footer

**Key section patterns:**

| Section | WebAnatomy Patterns (3-5) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Hero | Calculator/Estimator + Outcome Timeframe + Risk Reducer + Clear Value Proposition + Target Audience Clarity |
| Value Prop | Multiple Propositions + Outcome Quantification + Benefit Specificity |
| Pricing | Plan Clarity + Calculator/Estimator (echo) + Anchoring Tags + Risk Reducer + Easy Comparison |
| CTA | Friction Reduction + Consolidation + Microcopy Quality + Secondary Path |

**Palette + type:** White hero with a single soft-shadow card for the calculator. Violet (#7C3AED) input accents, green (#16A34A) for positive margin result. Outfit headings, Inter body.

**Interactive element (vanilla JS only):** Three inputs (unit cost EUR, shelf price EUR, weekly units sold). Live-updating output: margin % (colour-coded bar), weekly profit on this product, "Your whole shop could have hundreds of these. Get my free margin scan." All numbers labelled illustrative.

**Hero copy direction:** Headline: "What margin are you really making?" Subhead: "Try one product. Then imagine your whole shelf." Calculator card centred. CTA below result: "Get my free margin scan" + "Free. No card. Takes 2 minutes."

## RECIPE R5: "Owner's Path"

**Strategy:** Persona routing (single site / forecourt / multi-site). Tailors the value shown per visitor type. Use-case-framed features. Dual CTA (free scan + talk to us).

**Section stack:** Navbar > Hero (persona selector) > Tailored Value Prop (one per persona) > Features (use-case framed) > How It Works > Pricing > Proof > FAQ > Dual CTA > Footer

**Key section patterns:**

| Section | WebAnatomy Patterns (3-5) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Hero | Target Audience Clarity + Differentiation + Secondary Path + Visual Hierarchy + Risk Reducer |
| Features | Benefit-Led Copy + Pain-to-Outcome Mapping + Outcome Visualization + Use-Case Framing |
| Pricing | Plan Clarity + Easy Comparison + Anchoring Tags + Value-Based Naming + Risk Reducer |
| CTA | Friction Reduction + Secondary Path + Microcopy Quality + Consolidation |

**Palette + type:** Three accent colours mapped to personas (teal for single-site, violet for forecourt, amber for multi-site). Neutral base. Plus Jakarta Sans headings, Inter body.

**Interactive element (vanilla JS only):** Hero has three clickable cards ("I run one shop" / "I run a forecourt" / "I manage multiple sites"). Selection highlights that card and the value proposition section below morphs to show persona-specific pain + feature highlights. No page reload, CSS class toggle.

**Hero copy direction:** "Which best describes you?" with the three persona cards. Generic fallback headline visible before selection: "Find the money your shop leaves on the table." After selection, headline adapts (e.g. forecourt: "Fuel margins are fixed. Retail margins are not.").
